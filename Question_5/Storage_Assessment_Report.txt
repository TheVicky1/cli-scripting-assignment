6. Storage Health Observations

The available storage devices and mounted file systems were successfully detected.

The primary virtual disk has a total capacity of 20 GB. Its root partition has a usable capacity of 19 GB, of which 2.5 GB is used and 16 GB is available.

The root filesystem is operating at 14% disk utilization. This means approximately 86% of its capacity remains available.

The root filesystem has an inode utilization of 5%, with 2,382,200 free inodes. There is no immediate inode-exhaustion risk.

The /boot and /boot/efi partitions are mounted correctly and have low utilization levels of 16% and 7%, respectively.

Based on disk-space and inode statistics, no immediate storage-capacity issue was observed.

This assessment was performed inside an Ubuntu Multipass virtual machine. The commands confirm virtual storage capacity, mount status, disk utilization, and inode utilization. They do not confirm the physical SMART health of the Mac's underlying storage device.

7. Recommendations for Improving Storage Utilization

8. Maintain at least 15 to 20 percent free space on important file systems.

9. Monitor disk usage regularly using the df -hT command.

10. Monitor inode usage using df -i, especially for applications that create many small files.

11. Configure log rotation so that application and system logs do not grow without limits.

12. Remove obsolete temporary files, unused application data, cached packages, and old logs.

13. Archive or compress files that are not accessed regularly.

14. Configure monitoring alerts when disk-space or inode usage exceeds a threshold such as 80%.

15. Maintain regular backups of important application files, configuration data, and databases.

16. Review storage growth periodically and increase the virtual disk capacity before deploying storage-intensive workloads.

17. Store application logs, uploaded files, and database data in appropriately sized partitions or external storage when required.

Conclusion:

The available storage devices, mounted file systems, disk utilization, inode usage, and workspace storage consumption were successfully assessed.

The root filesystem currently uses 14% of its capacity and 5% of its available inodes. It has approximately 16 GB of free disk space and more than 2.38 million free inodes.

The current virtual server has sufficient storage capacity for a small application deployment. Regular monitoring, log rotation, cleanup, backups, and capacity planning should be performed to maintain reliable storage operation.
